About Us

Our Story


Beautiful Brainz is an initiative of young professional women aged 18-30, with a mission of supporting young girls aged 8-12 to stay and excel in school. According to the ministry of Education in Rwanda, the female school dropout rate was 6.8% in 2019. Some of the main causes of school dropouts are poverty, family conflicts, irresponsible parenting, being orphaned and family size.(CEC Journal).

Beautiful Brainz goal is to ensure that all girls enroll in school, stay and excel in school, by mitigating the severity of most of the major causes of school dropout. We support girls aged 8-12 from vulnerable families to access basic requirements needed by girls at school like school uniforms, shoes, books and sanitary materials to encourage girls to stay and excel in school .

Why Us

Our Vision

A World where every girl has access to quality education and takes the lead in designing a life of her dreams.

Our Mission

Empowering and supporting girls aged 8-12 from vulnerable families to stay and excel in school, dream big and work hard to turn their dreams into reality.

Our Work

Girls Education Fund

We support girls aged between 8-12, through providing basic requirements needed at school including ; school uniforms, shoes, books and sanitary materials. Through Beautiful Brainz Hub and Back to School Challenge, we are raising money to support young girls aged between 8-12 who dropped out of school or who are on the edge of dropping out due to lack of basic school requirements like school uniforms, shoes, books and sanitary materials.

Girl Child Mentorship

We train young girls to focus and prioritize their education, create positive values and voice their opinions. Furthermore we engage young women who are successful in their careers to inspire and motivate young girls.

Beautiful Brainz Hub (BBH)

An online platform that connects young professional women aged 18-30 who are interested in Technology, Leadership and Career Success. Within the Hub, they have access to virtual coaching and training to enhance their skills in technology, Leadership and career success. BBH Members teach digital skills to girls aged 8-12 from vulnerable communities, so that they grow up loving and more familiar with basic technology skills including; computer hardware ware(basics), basic MS.
